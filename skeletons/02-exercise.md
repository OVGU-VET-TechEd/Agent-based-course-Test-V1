## Summary
This 1-hour hands-on exercise session allows students to immediately apply all concepts learned in the lecture. Working with real research datasets, students will calculate descriptive statistics, create visualizations (histograms and box plots), and interpret their findings. The session emphasizes collaborative learning through pair work, practical troubleshooting, and sharing discoveries. This session directly builds on Session 01's theoretical foundation and transforms abstract concepts into concrete skills students can use in their own research projects.

**Connection to Agenda:** Second of two sessions; applies lecture concepts through practice.  
**Relevance:** Bridges theory and practice; builds confidence with real data analysis.  
**Didactics:** Hands-on workshop format with guided practice, peer collaboration, and instructor support.

## Activities
**Hands-On Exercises:**

- **Exercise 1: Descriptive Statistics Practice** (20 min)
  - Work in pairs to calculate statistics from dataset
  - Compare manual calculations with software output
  - Discuss when to use mean vs. median

- **Exercise 2: Visualization Challenge** (20 min)
  - Create histogram and box plot for assigned variables
  - Experiment with different bin sizes/parameters
  - Identify what each visualization reveals

- **Exercise 3: Real Research Application** (15 min)
  - Apply skills to mini research scenario
  - Choose appropriate statistics and visualizations
  - Present findings to another pair

- **Q&A and Troubleshooting** (throughout)
  - Common mistakes and how to fix them
  - Software tips and tricks
  - Clarifying confusing concepts from lecture
