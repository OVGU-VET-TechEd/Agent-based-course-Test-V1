## Modules / Sessions

### Session 01 â€“ Making Sense of Data: Central Tendency & Dispersion (Lecture)
**Type:** Lecture  
**Duration:** 120 minutes  
**Materials File:** `materials/01-lecture.md`

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the purpose of descriptive vs. inferential statistics
- Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
- Calculate and interpret measures of dispersion (variance, standard deviation, range)
- Identify the appropriate descriptive statistic for different data types (nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio)

**Summary:**
Here's where we get friendly with numbers! We'll start by understanding why we even bother with statistics (hint: it's about telling stories with data, not torturing students). Then we'll dive into the "Big Three" of central tendencyâ€”mean, median, and mode. I like to think of them as different ways of answering "What's typical here?" 

Next up: dispersion measures. These tell us if our data points are best friends huddling together or scattered across the universe. We'll tackle variance, standard deviation, and range using examples like test scores, salaries, and pizza delivery times (because why not?).

Finally, we'll crack the code on data types. Not all variables are created equal! We'll learn when to use which statistic depending on whether you're dealing with categories (like favorite ice cream flavors), rankings (like race positions), or actual numbers (like temperatures). By the end, you'll know exactly which tool to grab from your statistical toolbox.

Lots of examples, visual aids, and opportunities to ask "Wait, what?" when things get fuzzy. We're all learning together!

By the time we wrap up, you'll have created your own data visualizations and gained the confidence to describe any dataset that comes your way. Plus, you'll have practical examples you can immediately apply to your own research!
